G — Data Quality Notes

Normalization Assumptions

Flags & Methodology Notes
Vistar DSP field: Used the "Bidder Name" column as the DSP field, not "Buyer" as originally mapped. The Buyer column in Vistar contains a mix of agency names (Rapport, Nemo, Steel City Media) and numeric seat IDs that are not DSP-identifiable. Bidder Name gives consistent DSP names (The Trade Desk, StackAdapt, DV360, etc.). When Bidder Name = "Vistar," the DSP is labeled "Vistar (Managed)" - these are managed/agency buys intermediated through the Vistar platform.
Place Exchange currency: Revenue column is labeled "Gross Revenue (in Buyer Currency)." Amounts are treated as USD for aggregation, but some buyers may transact in other currencies. Flag any unusually high or low eCPMs on international buyers as potential FX discrepancies. Experian's $85,610 at $20 eCPM should be confirmed as USD.
Hivestack date aggregation: Each Hivestack file contains two date rows (current week: 2026-03-23 and 2026-03-30; prior week: 2026-03-16 and 2026-03-23). Both date rows were aggregated per advertiser within each weekly file. The 2026-03-23 date appears in both the current and prior week files - this is normal Hivestack export behavior and does not result in double-counting since aggregation is per-file.
Broadsign DSP splitting: Some advertisers appear under both "StackAdapt DSP" and "StackAdapt DSP - via Broadsign Ads." These are kept as separate DSP entries to preserve attribution accuracy. Revenue is summed at the advertiser level for spender rankings.
Date range misalignment across SSPs: Vistar's prior week report covers 3/14-3/20/2026; Hivestack's prior week covers 3/16-3/23/2026. This slight week boundary mismatch means some advertisers flagged as "new" or "churned" may reflect timing differences rather than true entry/exit. Use SSP-level details to confirm before acting on churn signals.
Advertiser name normalization: URL-format advertiser names were cleaned for display (e.g., libertymutual.com → Liberty Mutual, fernetbranca.com → Fernet-Branca). Approximately 30 advertiser names were normalized via lookup table. All remaining URL-format names are displayed as-is from source data. No revenue values were altered during normalization.
Maryland Reads eCPM anomaly: Maryland Reads (Vistar, BPI buyer) shows a very high eCPM of $45.57 in current week vs. $17.00 in prior week on a deal buy. This may reflect a deal rate renegotiation or a reporting artifact. Revenue is $1,221 current vs. $775 prior - worth verifying the deal terms haven't changed.
